## Step 2–3 — Logo audit: what the winners have in common

Grouping all 15 by structure reveals four recurring families:

**1. Motion gestures** (Nike, ASICS, HOKA, On) — a single dynamic stroke that implies speed or lift. Asymmetric, energetic.

**2. Geometric emblems** (Adidas, Converse, Reebok, Salomon) — symmetric, contained shapes that signal heritage and trust.

**3. Monogram letterforms** (New Balance, Under Armour, Skechers, On) — the brand's initials abstracted into a shape.

**4. Pictorial mascots** (Puma, Timberland, Crocs) — a recognizable creature or object.

### Shared DNA across all four families
- **Single unbroken shape** — 11 of 15 read as one continuous form.
- **One color, solid fill** — almost never gradient-dependent.
- **No fine detail** — every mark survives being shrunk to a heel tab or embossed/stitched.
- **Asymmetry = energy; symmetry = heritage** — a deliberate choice, not an accident.
- **Silhouette test** — each is recognizable in pure black at thumbnail size.

### The strategic gap
**No major footwear brand uses a South Asian visual language.** This is open territory. An Indian/Punjabi-rooted mark isn't a limitation — it is the single clearest path to differentiation in this market.

## Step 4–5 — Four concepts, each mapped to a proven family

### A · The Aankra  *(motion-gesture family)*
The hook-curl of Gurmukhi script, reimagined as a forward-leaning speed stroke. Occupies the same structural slot as the Swoosh while being unmistakably Punjabi. **Best for performance / streetwear positioning. Top recommendation.**

### B · The Mehraab  *(geometric-emblem family)*
The pointed arch of Mughal / Indo-Islamic architecture. Symmetric, premium, heritage-signalling — and it literally frames a space, which dovetails with the personalization concept (the customer's design lives inside the arch). **Best for luxury / craft positioning.**

### C · The Chann-N  *(monogram family)*
An "N" for Nishaan whose diagonal is a crescent moon — *chann*, a Punjabi term of endearment. A clear initial with a warm, ownable twist. **Most versatile; scales smallest.**

### D · The Punj  *(pictorial family)*
Five strokes for the five rivers of Punjab (the literal meaning of "Punj-ab"), converging to a point. The most story-rich mark. **Needs thickening to survive at small sizes.**

---

## Recommendation
Develop **The Aankra** as the primary mark — it behaves like the proven motion logos that dominate the category while drawing on a heritage no competitor owns. Hold **The Chann-N** as a flexible monogram alternative for small applications (insole stamp, lace tips, packaging).

### Suggested next steps
1. Refine the chosen mark's curve and weight.
2. Test it small (heel tab) and mirrored (left/right shoe).
3. Build 2–3 colorways.
4. Pair with a wordmark and define the tagline ("leave your mark").
5. Hand the vector to a designer or generate polished variations for final production.
